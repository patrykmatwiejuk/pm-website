Then I started to struggle with learning new things.
I needed some kind of impulse so I started looking
for bootcamps on the internet and I found a software testing one.
I was like, "what's that? Is this even a real thing worth looking into?"
Then I've started to read about software testing and I became
very interested in this topic.

I was always a perfectionist kind of person.

I have stopped being a complete perfectionist,
because it kills productivity, but I kept the care for the quality of things with me.
I could always spot bugs, minor and major errors in the UI or UX part
of an application, and I usually kept the fix suggestions for myself.
Not anymore! When I took a software testing course in the Coders Lab IT School,
it changed my point of view on things. I had a clear look on what I had yet to learn.
In the first three days we learned about testing methodologies
and everything included in the ISTQB syllabus.
I really liked that part because I learned a lot about the principles of testing.
We also had the opportunity to create our first user stories, test plans,
test cases and bug reports. I absolutely loved the SQL workshop part,
because I really like working with it.

On 18th of August 2019 I had the ISTQB FL exam which I passed.
It was hard even after reading the syllabus three times, but now I am a happy holder of the ISTQB FL certificate.

Now I'm looking for work in software testing.
